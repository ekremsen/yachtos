# MVP Roadmap

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Draft  
**Document Owner:** Product Team  
**Last Updated:** 29 June 2026

---

## Purpose

The purpose of the MVP is to validate the core value proposition of YachtOS.

The MVP is not intended to build every planned feature.

Its purpose is to answer one business question:

> Can YachtOS become the daily operational workspace for luxury yacht captains?

---

## MVP Goal

The MVP must help captains replace fragmented operational management with a single, reliable platform.

If captains continue using Excel, WhatsApp and personal notes after adopting YachtOS, the MVP has failed.

---

## Primary User

The MVP is built primarily for professional captains managing private luxury yachts.

Every product decision should prioritize the captain's daily workflow.

---

## Problems To Solve

The MVP should solve the following operational problems:

- Operational information is scattered across multiple tools.
- Important tasks are forgotten.
- Maintenance schedules are difficult to track.
- Inventory status is not always visible.
- Guest preferences rely on memory.
- Operational knowledge is difficult to share.
- Captains carry unnecessary mental workload.

---

## Success Criteria

The MVP will be considered successful if captains can:

- Organize operational information in one place.
- Replace spreadsheets for daily operations.
- Reduce forgotten operational tasks.
- Access important yacht information quickly.
- Trust YachtOS as their primary operational workspace.

---

## Product Principles

The MVP must be:

- Simple to learn
- Fast to use
- Mobile-friendly
- Reliable
- Easy to adopt
- Focused on real operational workflows

Every feature should reduce complexity, not introduce it.

---

## What The MVP Is Not

The MVP is not intended to become:

- A complete ERP system
- An accounting platform
- A marina management system
- An IoT monitoring platform
- A charter marketplace
- A feature-complete yacht management suite

The MVP should remain focused on solving the captain's daily operational challenges.

---

## Validation Strategy

Every major feature must be validated with real captains before implementation.

The product should evolve through continuous feedback from experienced professionals rather than assumptions.

---

## Exit Criteria

The MVP is complete when captains can confidently manage their daily yacht operations without depending on spreadsheets, WhatsApp conversations or personal memory.

At that point, YachtOS has successfully validated its product-market fit and can begin expanding into additional operational domains.

---

## MVP Principle

Build the smallest product that solves the biggest operational problem.
